BACKGROUND
The National Community Healthcare Agency has initiated a nationwide digital modernisation programme
intended to improve accessibility and efficiency across public healthcare clinics.
As part of this programme, the agency plans to introduce a Smart Healthcare Appointment Management
Platform that enables patients to schedule medical appointments, receive appointment reminders, access
basic health notices, submit medical assistance requests, and track clinic queue status through a unified web-
based portal.
During a preliminary technical review, the agency identified that its current infrastructure is fragmented, lacks
centralised  monitoring  capabilities,  and  does  not  meet  modern  security  and  operational  resilience
expectations required for handling healthcare-related information and public-facing services.
The agency therefore requires a secure and operational environment capable of supporting the new platform
while  maintaining  system  availability,  administrative  accountability,  and  recoverability  during  operational
incidents or system failures.
Your organisation has been engaged as a cyber engineering consultancy to design and implement the
required solution.
You have been assigned as the lead Cyber Engineering Analyst responsible for delivering a production-ready
environment.

Engineering-First Philosophy
Unlike  traditional  cybersecurity  competitions,  no  infrastructure,  software  products,  deployment
templates, reference architectures, or predefined technology stacks will be provided.
Participants  are  expected  to  independently  determine  suitable  operating  systems,  deployment
approaches, security mechanisms, monitoring solutions, backup strategies, and operational controls
based on sound engineering judgement and industry practices.
The objective of this challenge is not to assess familiarity with any specific vendor or product, but rather
to  evaluate  the  participant’s  ability  to  independently  design,  implement,  secure,  operate,  monitor,
troubleshoot, document, and recover a system using practical cyber engineering principles.
COMPETITION DURATION
Participants will be given three (3) weeks to complete the challenge.
During  this  period,  participants  may  develop  and  deploy  their  solution  using  any  environment  of  their
choosing.
This  may  include  cloud  infrastructure,  local  virtualisation  platforms,  containerised  environments,  physical
hardware, or hybrid deployment approaches.
All provisioning activities, software installation, configuration, security implementation, testing, troubleshooting,
operational validation, documentation, and demonstration preparation are the responsibility of the participant.
PROJECT REQUIREMENTS
The Healthcare Agency requires a publicly accessible web-based platform capable of supporting appointment
and patient engagement services.
The  chosen  solution  must  be  deployed  on  an  operating  system  selected  by  the  participant  and  must
incorporate appropriate security measures to protect system availability, confidentiality, and integrity.
The deployed environment must demonstrate secure administrative access mechanisms and effective user
management practices.
Participants are expected to implement controls that minimise the likelihood of unauthorised access while
maintaining operational usability for legitimate administrators and users.
The solution should demonstrate evidence of secure baseline configuration practices and system hardening
activities appropriate for production-facing services.
The application environment must include a database component capable of storing appointment records,
user account information, and service-related data.

Participants  are  expected  to  implement  suitable  database  protection  measures  and  demonstrate  an
understanding  of  least-privilege  access  principles,  credential  security,  access  management,  and  secure
service configuration.
The agency has also identified a requirement for operational visibility and event awareness.
Participants are therefore required to implement logging, monitoring, or observability capabilities that enable
administrators to identify system activity, authentication events, operational issues, and potentially security-
relevant incidents.
The technologies and approaches selected to fulfil this requirement are entirely at the discretion of the
participant.
In addition, the agency requires assurance that critical services and operational data can be restored in the
event of accidental deletion, service failure, or infrastructure disruption.
Participants must therefore design and implement an appropriate backup and recovery approach capable of
restoring business-critical functionality and data.
Participants  are  encouraged  to  consider  maintainability,  operational  simplicity,  and  practical  recovery
procedures throughout the design and implementation process.
DELIVERABLES
At the conclusion of the challenge, participants shall submit a technical package containing sufficient evidence
to demonstrate the design, implementation, security, operation, monitoring, and recoverability of their solution.
The submission should include:
Solution architecture diagram
Technical implementation documentation
Security configuration evidence
Administrative access controls documentation
Monitoring and logging evidence
Backup and recovery procedures
Validation or testing evidence
Supporting artefacts relevant to the solution
Evidence may include screenshots, configuration files, command outputs, logs, scripts, validation results,
diagrams, deployment notes, or any additional material considered relevant by the participant.
Participants should assume that assessors have not previously seen their environment.
Documentation should therefore be sufficiently detailed to allow assessors to understand the participant’s
engineering decisions, implementation methodology, security considerations, and operational approach.

LIVE DEMONSTRATION
Each participant or team will be allocated thirty (30) minutes for a live demonstration and technical defence
session.
During this session, participants will be expected to present their architecture and explain the reasoning
behind  their  deployment  decisions,  security  controls,  monitoring  implementation,  backup  strategy,  and
operational design choices.
Participants must demonstrate that their solution is functional and capable of supporting the stated business
requirements.
Assessors may request demonstrations involving:
Administrative access controls
User management processes
Security configurations
Database protections
Monitoring and logging functionality
Backup validation
Recovery procedures
Troubleshooting activities
The demonstration should also provide evidence that participants understand the engineering decisions they
have made throughout the project lifecycle.
Assessors  may  ask  participants  to  discuss  trade-offs,  explain  alternative  approaches,  justify  technology
selections, and describe how operational or security risks were mitigated.
Participants should be prepared to defend both their technical implementation and the reasoning behind their
engineering decisions.
ASSESSMENT PHILOSOPHY
The JURUS programme adopts an engineering-first assessment philosophy.
Assessment will therefore focus on demonstrated capability rather than theoretical knowledge alone.
Participants will be evaluated based on:
Quality of implementation
Effectiveness of security controls
Operational usability and maintainability
Completeness and clarity of documentation
Monitoring and visibility capabilities

A more complex solution will not necessarily achieve a higher score. Greater emphasis will be placed on
solutions that are appropriate, secure, maintainable, well-documented, operationally practical, and clearly

The highest-scoring submissions will demonstrate not only technical competence, but also sound engineering

EXPECTED OUTCOME
Upon completion of the challenge, successful participants should demonstrate the capability to independently
design, build, secure, monitor, troubleshoot, document, and recover a production-ready system using industry-
relevant cyber engineering practices.
This outcome aligns directly with the JURUS Level 1 Analyst competency objective of developing practitioners
capable of independently building and securing operational systems while applying structured troubleshooting
methodologies and sound operational discipline.
